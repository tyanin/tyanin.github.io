About Me

Hello! I'm Tyanin Opdahl, a passionate Computer Science student at Western Washington University pursuing both my MS and BS degrees simultaneously. My research focuses on disability fairness and intersectionality in machine learning systems, and I'm dedicated to creating technology that serves all users equitably.

My work sits at the intersection of accessibility, data science, and advocacy. Through my research, I explore how we can ensure machine learning systems are fair for individuals with disabilities, taking an intersectional approach that considers the complex ways different identities interact with technology barriers.

Beyond technical research, I'm deeply committed to equity and inclusion in STEM. As a department ambassador, strategic planning task force member, and outreach officer, I work to break down barriers and create more inclusive spaces in computer science education and industry.

Fake News Detection Using NLP Models

Natural Language Processing Neural Networks Comparative Analysis Team Project

Explored the efficacy of three natural language processing models - Neural Bag of Words (NBoW), Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM), and Transformer networks - in classifying news articles as fake or real.

Key Finding: Models performed with nearly equivalent capabilities, contradicting expectations that Transformers would significantly outperform LSTM and NBoW models.
